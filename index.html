Preventive Measures

keep your place clean

  • Every Home Is Different, But Common High-Touch Surfaces Include: Door Handles, Tables, Chairs, Kitchen And Bathroom surfaces.
  • Sanitize These With Disinfectant,sprays And Water.

maintain distance

  • What To Do To Keep Yourself And Others Safe From COVID-19?
  • Well, Maintain At Least A 2-Metre Distance Between You And Others To minimize the Risk Of Infection When They Cough, Sneeze Or Speak.

don't touch face

  • It’s a quirk of human nature that we touch our eyes, nose and mouth all day long. It’s also a major way we pick up infections like COVID-19.
  • To Minimize The Spread Of The Novel Coronavirus, W.H.O Recommended, Not to Touch one's Face Because The Virus Can Be Transmitted Through Mucous Membranes In The Mouth, Nose And Eyes.

Sanitize your hands

  • Frequent And Rigorous Hand Sanitizing Is One Of The Most Common Preventive Measure.
  • During the global pandemic, one of the cheapest, easiest and most significant ways to prevent the spread of a virus is to wash your hands frequently with soap and water.

use napkin

  • WHO Suggests Everyone to Cough Or Sneeze Into Elbows.
  • If Not That!
  • Simply Cover Your Nose And Mouth With A Handkerchief While Sneezing Or Coughing.

wear a mask

  • Masks And Face Coverings Can Prevent From Transmitting The COVID-19 Virus To Others And Will Provide Protection To The One Wearing it.
  • Multiple Studies Have Shown That Face Coverings Can Contain Droplets Expelled From The Wearer, Which Are Responsible For The Transmission Of The Virus.

things you should DO

Sanitize your hand

  • Soap and water work to remove all types of germs from hands, while sanitizer acts by killing certain germs on the skin.
  • Handwashing reduces the amounts of all types of germs, pesticides, and metals on hands.
  • Washing hands can keep you healthy and prevent the spread of respiratory and diarrheal infections from one person to the next

always wear a mask

Make sure you always wear your mask with the colored side facing outwards. Carefully cover your nose, mouth and chin, and make sure there is no gap between your face and the mask. Do not touch the mask with your hands while wearing it.

stay home during fever

If a person with fever stays in a a cool room, by convection method temperature may reduce, that’s why fever patients are kept in aircon rooms. But if you are feeling cold you pull over a thick blanket on yourself. It’ll comfort you but the core temperature may linger on a little longer. Same principle with tepid sponging of body with water.

things you should NOT DO

avoid close contact with animals

Avoid close contact and unprotected with live or dead farm or wild animals. When visiting live markets in areas currently experiencing cases of novel coronavirus, avoid direct unprotected contact with live animals and surfaces in contact with animals; The consumption of raw or undercooked animal products should be avoided. Raw meat, milk or animal organs should be handled with care, to avoid cross-contamination with uncooked foods, as per good food safety practices.

avoid close contact with people

Avoid close contact with someone who is sick; Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces . When sharing, please include accompanying text to complete the message. COVID-19 spreads primarily from person to person. This can be via droplets released when people sick with the new coronavirus cough or sneeze. It can also spread when you’re in close contact with human. so, avoid close contact.

avoid crowded area

Reduce Interaction Avoid Crowded Areas When in a group, a maximum of 5 persons. Do not intermingle between groups, and areas that have been cordoned off. and keep 1 metre between groups.
